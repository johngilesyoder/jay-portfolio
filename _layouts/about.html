Design

"It’s not just what it looks like and feels like. Design is how it works."

As a designer, every intention and focus should be on the holistic experience. Aesthetic is a single component of good design, which extends to experience and usability. I approach every project and feature with the responsibility of how useful, delightful, and intuitive the experience and/or interface will be to a real human being.

If you're interested in my workflow or knowing some of the tools I use to create design, check out my Resources page where you can find my tools of choice (including Sketch and InVision).

Development

I love taking designs (mine or others') from static mockups to fully immersive products. I have been a UI developer in addition to a designer for almost 9 years. I approach development with the same design ethos. My current focus is with React and Styled Components, but I have considerable experience with SASS, PHP, jQuery, Thymeleaf, and Liquid. I have also built and maintained complex projects with CMS's such as Wordpress, Drupal, Shopify, and Squarespace.

I would argue that development is also design. Establishing sustainable, extensible, and readable front-end systems is one of the most satisfying parts of the trade.

Creating Value

Having experience in a diverse assemblage of business environments (from startups to enterprise-level companies) has afforded me a very useful perspective of how to create value — and how crucial it is at any stage of a business. I'm not just here to design or to write fancy code. I'm here to solve problems and execute the vision as efficiently and agile as is reasonable. Excellence should precipitate execution.

One of the ways I enjoy maximizing value is by being able to cover a large spectrum of capability — concept to design to UI development implementation. Often, I fill several positions as one resource which can be cost-effective and provides a highly streamlined and agile workflow. Being able to bridge the gap between design and development also has unique advantages in terms of designing for technological requirements and articulating communication between departments.
